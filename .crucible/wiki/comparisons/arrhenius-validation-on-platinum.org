The experimental measurements of CO oxidation on Pt(111) provide
a direct validation of the Arrhenius relationship for this system.
The measured activation energy of 1.05 eV, obtained from a linear
Arrhenius plot across the 300-600K range, is consistent with both
DFT predictions and literature values.

** Consistency Between Theory and Experiment

The agreement between the measured activation energy and
computational predictions citep:smith2024 supports the use of DFT
for screening catalyst candidates. The linearity of the Arrhenius
plot across the full temperature range suggests a single
rate-limiting step, simplifying kinetic modeling.

** Implications for Catalyst Design

This validation strengthens the case for using computed activation
energies as descriptors in catalyst screening workflows. See
[[file:../concepts/activation-energy.org][Activation Energy]] for
the theoretical background.
