* Activation Energy
:PROPERTIES:
:ARTICLE_TYPE: concept
:SOURCE_KEYS: test-notebook-entry
:STATUS: draft
:CREATED: 2026-04-03
:END:

Activation energy is the minimum energy required for a chemical
reaction to proceed. In heterogeneous catalysis, it determines
the temperature dependence of reaction rates through the
Arrhenius equation.

** Measurement on Pt(111)

Recent experiments on CO oxidation over Pt(111) measured an
activation energy of 1.05 eV across the 300-600K temperature
range, consistent with DFT predictions citep:smith2024.

See also [[file:../methods/arrhenius-analysis.org][Arrhenius Analysis]].

** Relationship to Catalyst Design

The activation energy is a key descriptor for catalyst screening.
Lower activation energies generally indicate more active catalysts,
though selectivity must also be considered.
